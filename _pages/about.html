I'm a Product Manager with an engineering background who’s driven by curiosity—always asking how things work, and more importantly, how they can work better for people. My path has been anything but linear: from building computer vision systems to running my own startup, each step has deepened my understanding of how ideas become products.

My journey began in computer science, where I learned to think in systems and build from first principles. But I was always more interested in the “why” behind the code—the user, the context, the outcome. That curiosity led me to co-found a tech-enabled café startup. Building something from scratch and eventually navigating its acquisition taught me more about resilience, iteration, and customer understanding than any classroom could. It was, in many ways, a real-world MBA.

Those lessons shaped how I see products today. Pursuing a Master’s in Product Design and Management at IIIT Hyderabad helped me bridge technical execution with strategic thinking. Since then, I’ve worked on everything from industrial automation software to health-tech research—always focusing on reducing ambiguity and moving from insight to implementation.

At the core, I want to build technology that helps people solve real problems. I bring the lens of an engineer, the mindset of a builder, and the discipline of a product manager—working to create systems and products that quietly make life a little easier.
