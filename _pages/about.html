I'm a Product Manager with a background in engineering, and I love figuring out how things work so I can make them work better for people. My journey has been a bit unique—I've gone from building computer vision systems from scratch to launching my own startup. That experience gave me a firsthand look at what it truly takes to turn a cool idea into a real-world product.

My career started with a deep dive into computer science, but I was always more interested in the 'why' behind the code. That curiosity led me to co-found a tech-enabled cafe. Building a business from the ground up, and eventually navigating its acquisition, was a crash course in what really matters: understanding customers, adapting on the fly, and making tough calls. It was the most practical MBA I could have asked for.

That experience solidified my path into product. I pursued a Master's in Product Design and Management to connect my hands-on technical skills with the strategy and user empathy needed to build great products. This is where I've had the chance to shape everything from industrial software to go-to-market strategies for new health-tech.

Ultimately, I want to build technology that helps people solve real problems. I'm looking for a role where I can use my background as an engineer, my experience as a founder, and my passion for product to create things that truly make a difference.
